Kolkata (Calcutta) – The cultural heart of India, known for its colonial architecture, vibrant art scene, bustling markets, iconic Howrah Bridge, and historic landmarks like Victoria Memorial and Indian Museum. Darjeeling Nestled in the Himalayas, this hill station is famous for its world-renowned tea, stunning sunrise views from Tiger Hill, the heritage Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (Toy Train), and the majestic Kanchenjunga range. Sundarbans The world’s largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its rich biodiversity, including the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger and saltwater crocodiles. Gangasagar A sacred pilgrimage site where the Ganges meets the Bay of Bengal, known for the annual Makar Sankranti festival. Chandannagar A former French colony with a rich blend of French and Bengali cultures, known for its riverside promenade and historic buildings. Bakkhali A quiet seaside retreat with clean beaches, fresh seafood, and stunning views of the Bay of Bengal. Shantiniketan Founded by Rabindranath Tagore, this quaint town is known for its artistic vibe, Visva-Bharati University, and vibrant cultural festivals like Poush Mela. Bishnupur Famous for its stunning terracotta temples, traditional Baluchari sarees, and rich musical heritage. Siliguri A major commercial hub and gateway to the Northeast, known for its lush tea estates and the Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary. Digha A popular beach town on the Bay of Bengal, perfect for a weekend getaway with its golden sands, seafood delicacies, and stunning sunsets.
